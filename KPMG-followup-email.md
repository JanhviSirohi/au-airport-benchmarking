Following delivery of the Australian airport investment and expenditure benchmarking report (FY20–FY25, 10 airports), I wanted to outline the enhancements that would materially strengthen the analysis in a follow-up engagement. I have grouped them by analytical leverage and indicated the data-acquisition or methodology effort required.

The current report covers the four ACCC-monitored airports (Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Perth), Adelaide (FY22–FY25 audited), the Queensland Airports Limited group (FY20–FY24, group-level), and program-total disclosures for Hobart, Canberra, Darwin and Cairns. The strongest series are the ACCC supplementary database for SYD/MEL/BNE/PER and the AAL audited cash-flow CAPEX line for ADL. Six methodological caveats (F3, F4, F5, F6, F8, F9 in the audit) limit the precision of cross-airport comparisons; a follow-up scope would address these directly.

## Tier 1 — High-leverage data acquisition

**1. ASIC company extracts for the four private holding companies**
Hobart International Airport Pty Ltd, Capital Airport Group Pty Ltd, Northern Territory Airports Pty Ltd, North Queensland Airports Pty Ltd. Audited consolidated annual financial statements are typically lodged with ASIC but require paid extract requests. This unlocks per-FY P&L, CAPEX, and EBITDA for the four currently-unregulated airports, eliminating the need to rely on indicative program totals.
- Estimated cost: A$1,200–1,500 in ASIC fees plus broker margin if using InfoTrack / CITEC Confirm
- Effort: 1 week from extract receipt to integration into the per-FY framework

**2. Adelaide Airport Limited FY20 and FY21 standalone Annual Financial Reports**
A direct request to AAL Investor Relations (`ir@adelaideairport.com.au`) typically secures these. They would close the ADL series back to pre-COVID, enabling a clean 6-year build-cycle trajectory and proper FY20-baselined recovery analysis.
- Estimated cost: nil (direct request)
- Effort: 2–3 days post-receipt

**3. International peer benchmarks — Auckland Airport, Singapore Changi, Auckland Airport's NZX-listed group**
Auckland Airport (AIA.NZ) publishes audited annuals on a 30-June fiscal year, directly comparable to Australian airports. Changi Airport Group publishes annually with split aero/non-aero detail. This contextualises whether MEL's FY24 CAPEX/revenue of 68.2% is "globally high" or "AU build-cycle normal" — a question that cannot be answered within the AU-only cohort.
- Estimated cost: nil (public)
- Effort: 1 week

## Tier 2 — Methodology refinement

**4. Real-terms restatement using ABS CPI**
ABS CPI cumulative growth FY20–FY25 was approximately 22%; ADL's "5.4× CAPEX ramp" includes ~12% nominal CPI. A real-terms restatement (FY20-base or FY24-base) would clarify whether multi-year capital intensity trajectories are real or nominal.
- Estimated cost: nil (ABS data)
- Effort: 2–3 days

**5. CAPEX-by-purpose split — capacity expansion vs maintenance vs commercial development**
Master plans for SYD, MEL, BNE, PER and HBA disclose multi-year CAPEX schedules broken out by purpose (typically: airfield, terminal, landside, commercial property, sustainability). Cross-referencing these against audited cash-flow CAPEX would distinguish growth investment from steady-state renewal.
- Estimated cost: nil (master plans are public)
- Effort: 2 weeks for full ten-airport coverage; HBA Master Plan 2022 (>10 MB) and BNE Master Plan 2020 are the largest documents

**6. QAL group disaggregation — Gold Coast standalone**
A direct request to QAL Investor Relations for the aero/non-aero revenue split, CAPEX cash-flow line, and Gold Coast Airport segment financials. QAL group consolidates four airports; the lack of standalone Gold Coast figures is the single largest reason QAL is not directly comparable to the ACCC-monitored cohort.
- Estimated cost: nil (direct request)
- Effort: dependent on QAL response time

**7. Bond prospectuses for any of HIAPL / CBR / NQA / ADG that have issued listed debt**
Where airport holding companies have issued AUD MTN programs or listed bonds, the offering prospectuses typically contain three years of audited financials with detail beyond annual reports. Subscription access to S&P Capital IQ, Bloomberg or Refinitiv would identify what is outstanding.
- Estimated cost: depends on existing client subscriptions
- Effort: 1 week

## Tier 3 — Analytical extensions

**8. Forward-looking traffic and CAPEX forecasts to FY30**
Master plans publish traffic forecasts to 2040–2043 horizons. Layering forward CAPEX schedules against forward traffic forecasts would produce per-pax investment intensity projections, useful for understanding which airports are over- or under-investing relative to forecast demand.

**9. Regulated asset base (RAB) trajectory analysis**
ACCC supplementary database carries asset-base data for the four monitored airports back to FY07-08. A long-horizon RAB-growth analysis would contextualise FY24 CAPEX intensity against multi-decade investment patterns.

**10. Cross-validation against airline disclosures**
Qantas and Virgin annual reports disclose airport-by-airport landing charges paid in some years. Cross-referencing these against ACCC-published aero revenue per passenger would provide a third-party check on airport-disclosed pricing.

## Recommendation

For a focused follow-up engagement, Tier 1 items (1) and (2) — ASIC extracts for the four private holdcos plus the AAL FY20/FY21 reports — would deliver the largest analytical uplift, transforming half the cohort from "program-total disclosure" to "per-FY audited" status. Tier 2 items (4) and (5) — real-terms restatement and CAPEX-by-purpose split — would address the highest-impact methodological caveats in the current report.

A combined Tier 1 + Tier 2 scope is consistent with a 4–5 week engagement at standard rates, with the deliverable being an updated report covering all 10 airports on a per-FY audited basis (subject to ASIC extract availability) plus an extended methodology section.
