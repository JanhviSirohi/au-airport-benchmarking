# Australian Airport Benchmarking — Raw Data
Accessed: 2026-04-27
All currency AUD nominal unless otherwise noted. Australian fiscal year ends 30 Jun.

## Conventions and notes
- Financial figures for ACCC-monitored airports (SYD, MEL, BNE, PER) come from the ACCC Airport Monitoring Report 2024-25 supplementary database (XLSX, published 5 March 2026), which contains audited regulatory account series FY07-08 through FY24-25. These are aeronautical-services + non-aeronautical services as defined in Schedule 2 of the Airports Act and may differ from group-consolidated annual report figures by intercompany eliminations, intangibles treatment, "line in the sand" (LIS) adjustments, and treatment of non-airport entities (Sydney Airport's transition from listed-trust to unlisted post-2022).
- Operational figures (passengers, ATM, freight) come from BITRE annual financial-year file `web_airports_annual_fy_2024_25.xlsx`, which covers RPT operations only; FY24-25 figures are also included for forward reference.
- For Tier-2 airports (ADL, CBR, OOL, HBA, DRW, CNS), full multi-year financial breakdowns are not consistently published. Where individual financial reports were located, data is recorded; where not, cells are stamped `N/A — not located on 2026-04-27`.
- Sydney FY24 audited financial statements show a large negative profit driven by a one-off non-cash interest line (~A$9.6 bn, related to deferred consideration / unitholder distribution following the 2022 take-private). EBITDA on regulated services basis is the operating-profit line below.
- Where two sources (ACCC vs annual report) conflict by >2%, both are listed and flagged.

## Cross-cutting reconciliation notes

1. ACCC vs BITRE pax totals for the four monitored airports are within 1-2% in most years, except Perth (BITRE excludes the substantial FIFO charter activity captured by ACCC). 
2. ACCC vs BITRE aircraft movements: ACCC is total movements (all RPT + GA + charter); BITRE is RPT-only. Difference is largest for Perth (FIFO) and Brisbane (GA training).
3. Sydney FY24 audited statements show a A$9.6 bn negative interest line — this is a non-cash unitholder distribution recognition tied to the 2022 take-private debt restructuring and does not reflect operating cash flow. Use the operating-profit / EBITDA line instead.
4. QAL is a four-airport group; standalone Gold Coast financials are not separately published. Rough allocation (GCA passengers as % of group): GCA ~77% of group pax FY24 — an analyst could pro-rate group financials but this is not a primary disclosure.
5. Perth FY24 ACCC figures are revised per the September 2025 errata XLSX; the supplementary database used here reflects revised values.
6.
