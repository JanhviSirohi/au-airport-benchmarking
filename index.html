OPEX/pax tightly clustered in FY25. SYD A$21 · MEL A$20 · BNE A$18 · PER A$24 · ADL A$15. Cohort median A$20. BNE remains the lowest of the ACCC-4, reflecting RPT-weighted throughput on a modern parallel-runway base. PER is the highest per-pax, partly reflecting FIFO-charter operating cost and remoteness.

Sydney aero/pax premium is structural. A$29.43/pax FY25 (1.40× cohort median A$21). Drivers: (i) slot-constrained gateway → higher per-pax landing yield; (ii) 40% international pax share (highest of cohort) → international landing charges materially higher than domestic. Aero share of revenue: 61.1% FY25 (up from 52.7% FY20).
ADL underlying EBITDA margin of 60.6% is comparable to ACCC scope; statutory 81.8% is not. AAL statutory EBITDA includes A$67.6m FY25 fair-value gain on investment properties; underlying EBITDA strips this out. EBITDA/pax (less scope-sensitive than margin) is the cleanest comparator: ADL A$22 vs SYD A$27, BNE A$26, PER A$21, MEL A$18.

6. Methodology & Caveats

Scope, sources, and key incomparabilities

Scope: 10 Australian airports — 4 ACCC-monitored gateways (SYD/MEL/BNE/PER) + 6 capital-city or capital-adjacent airports (ADL, OOL/QAL, CBR, HBA, DRW, CNS). Financial ratios computable for SYD/MEL/BNE/PER (full) + ADL (FY23–FY24) + QAL group (FY20–FY24, group-level only).

Fiscal year basis: Australian financial year ending 30 June. FY24 = year ended 30 Jun 2024.

Currency: AUD nominal — no inflation adjustment. ABS CPI cumulative FY20–FY24 ≈ 18%, so multi-year averages crossing the pandemic should be read as nominal trends.

Primary sources:

  • ACCC Airport Monitoring Report 2023-24 supplementary database (FY07-08 to FY23-24 time series for SYD/MEL/BNE/PER)
  • BITRE annual airport activity file (passengers, ATMs, freight for all 10 airports)
  • AAL Annual Financial Reports FY24, FY25 (Adelaide)
  • QAL Annual Reports FY20–FY24 (Gold Coast group)
  • Productivity Commission inquiry submissions (HBA sub031, NTA sub008, NQA sub049, CBR sub056)
  • Master plans, NAIF disclosures, ministerial press releases, corporate project pages

Key incomparabilities:

  • Regulated vs unregulated: only the four ACCC airports have a public regulatory-account series; CBR/HBA/DRW/CNS have no public audited annual P&L (program disclosures only).
  • Build-out vs steady-state: FY20 captures BNE post-runway hangover; FY24 captures MEL's third-runway and PER's terminal CAPEX pulse.
  • COVID-distorted years FY20 (half affected), FY21 (full collapse), FY22 (partial recovery). Per-pax ratios in these years are mechanically inflated by denominator collapse.
  • QAL group reporting: Gold Coast standalone P&L not disclosed across all 4 QAL annual reports (2022–2025); QAL group consolidates Gold Coast + Townsville + Mt Isa + Longreach (Gold Coast ~77% of QAL group pax FY24). 11-year QAL P&L series FY15–FY25 available; project-level Gold Coast capex disclosed (A$500m precinct, A$260m terminal, A$17m ETROO).
  • Perth ACCC pax includes FIFO charter; BITRE pax is RPT-only — gap is large (FY24 ACCC 16.14m vs BITRE 12.79m, 26%). PER per-pax ratios use the ACCC denominator throughout to match financial scope.
  • Sydney FY24 statutory P&L distorted by A$9.6bn one-off non-cash unitholder distribution post-2022 take-private. Report uses ACCC "operating profit excl. landfill" (FY24: A$1,083.83m) as EBITDA throughout — never the statutory line.
  • ADL FY20–FY22 financials missing — AAL FY24 report carries FY24 + FY23 only; older PDFs return 403/404 to anonymous fetches. ADL 5y averages are FY23–FY24 only.
Data provenance — live vs training data

Every figure in the underlying files traces to a live URL fetched on 2026-04-27 — none came from model training data recall. The user's standing rule is "no training-data figures; verify with live sources; stamp access dates" — applied throughout.

Provenance breakdown:

  • ~85% primary live fetches: ACCC XLSX, BITRE XLSX, AAL/QAL annual report PDFs, NAIF page, PC submission PDFs (parsed locally with pypdf after WebFetch returned encoded streams).
  • ~10% near-primary: Hobart Master Plan 2022 figures via Australian Aviation / Tasmanian Times secondary citations (PDF >10MB exceeded WebFetch limit; figures cross-checked against multiple secondary live sources).
  • ~5% via WebSearch result snippets: Some ministerial press releases (minister.infrastructure.gov.au) timed out on direct fetch; figures captured from search-result excerpts, which are live but less direct than full HTML retrieval.

Things flagged as not located: CBR/HBA/DRW/CNS audited annual P&L line-items, ADL FY20–FY22 financials, ADL FY24 CAPEX cash-flow line, QAL aero/non-aero split. These are real gaps, not concealed estimates.

Top accuracy risks (F1–F16) — click any code to open its full risk card
  • F3 Pax denominator scope (high) — PER uses ACCC pax incl FIFO; ADL uses BITRE; QAL uses group pax. Cross-airport per-pax ratios reflect different denominator scopes.
  • F4 EBITDA definition (high) — ACCC operating-profit-excl-landfill, AAL underlying EBITDA (excl FV gain), and QAL group EBITDA are scope-distinct measures. EBITDA/pax is the lower-variance comparator.
  • F5 Revenue scope (medium) — ACCC regulated-account revenue is narrower than AAL total revenue (which includes property businesses).
  • F6 Nominal vs real terms (medium) — figures are AUD nominal; ADL "5.4× ramp" includes ~12% CPI over FY22–FY25; real-terms ramp ≈ 4.8×.
  • F7 5y avg method (medium) — "cumulative-weighted" (Σ CAPEX / Σ pax) vs arithmetic mean of annual ratios can differ ~10–20% on COVID-distorted years.
  • F8 Cohort median sensitivity (medium) — n=4 (ACCC-monitored) median CAPEX/pax = A$13.5; n=5 (incl. ADL) median = A$11.5. Both shown in league table.
  • F9 Indicative program totals (medium) — HBA A$350m is sum of 4 named projects in mixed lifecycle stages; ADL A$1bn is the "indicative" 10-year program disclosure; CBR A$3bn includes adjacent commercial property.
  • F11 Source discrepancy reconciliation (medium) — PER pax 16.14m (ACCC) vs 12.79m (BITRE); ADL pax 8.54m (AAL) vs 8.25m (BITRE); BNE ATM 222.5k (ACCC) vs 188k (BITRE RPT-only). Denominator choice documented per ratio.
  • F14 Non-recurring item normalisation (medium) — FY21 JobKeeper, FY21 ADL Virgin debt recovery, ADL FV gains FY22/24/25, SYD FY24 A$9.6bn one-off non-cash unitholder distribution. Major items normalised; FY21 JobKeeper not normalised at the cohort level.
