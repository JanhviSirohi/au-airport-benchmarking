Accuracy risks (F1–F16)

F1PDF extraction completenessMed
IssueFor audited financial reports, the cash-flow statement line "Payments for property, plant and equipment" is the authoritative CAPEX figure — distinct from "capital commitments" disclosed in notes (forward-looking, not actual outlay).
MethodologyFor every primary-source PDF, the cash-flow statement and revenue note are read in full and reconciled against summary tables in the directors' report.
ResidualQAL group annual reports may contain aero/non-aero split and CAPEX detail in notes not in scope of this pass.
Affects claims: C008 (verified) · C012 (potential further detail in QAL notes)
F2Source-document size considerationsMed
IssueLarge primary PDFs (>10 MB) — including HBA Master Plan 2022 and ADG Aviation Green Paper submission Nov 2023 — require local extraction via curl + pypdf rather than browser-style fetch.
StatusAAL Integrated Review FY22 (16 MB) extracted in full. HBA Master Plan 2022 not yet extracted; HBA program-total figure (C005) currently sourced from per-project pages and ministerial press releases.
Affects claims: C005 (precision could be refined with full master plan extraction)
F3Pax denominator inconsistency across cohortHigh
IssueDifferent airports use different passenger denominators in their per-pax ratios.
Detail PER: ACCC pax incl FIFO (16.14m FY24)
SYD/MEL/BNE: ACCC pax matching financial scope
ADL: BITRE RPT pax (8.25m FY24); AAL itself reports 8.54m incl transit/transfer
QAL group: group pax (8.34m FY24, 4 airports consolidated)
ImplicationCross-airport per-pax ratios are NOT strictly comparable. PER's A$23 OPEX/pax vs ADL's A$13 includes denominator effect.
Affects claims: C004 C007 C008 C012
F4EBITDA definition driftHigh
Issue"EBITDA" means three different things across the cohort.
Detail ACCC-4: "operating profit excl landfill" from supp DB
ADL: "underlying EBITDA" (excl FV gain on investment properties; A$41.6m FY24, A$67.6m FY25)
QAL group: group EBITDA incl property/development gains; 4-airport scope
MitigationBest practice: report EBITDA/pax (less scope-sensitive than margin) for cross-airport comparisons. ADL statutory margin (75.5% FY24) shown only with explicit asterisk.
Affects claims: C006 C012 C013 C020
F5Revenue scope differenceMed
IssueACCC monitoring captures revenue from regulated services (aero + commercial on regulated airport site). AAL/QAL/private corporate accounts capture all entity revenue, which may include non-airport property.
ImplicationRevenue-denominator ratios (CAPEX/rev, OPEX/rev, EBITDA margin) are not strictly comparable across regulated and unregulated airports.
Affects claims: C001 C002 C022
F6Nominal vs real termsMed
IssueAll figures AUD nominal — no CPI adjustment. ABS CPI cumulative: FY20-FY24 ~18%, FY20-FY25 ~22%, FY22-FY25 ~12%.
ExampleADL "5.4× CAPEX ramp FY22→FY25" (C003) includes ~12% CPI; real ramp ≈ 4.8×.
Affects claims: C003 · all multi-year averages
F75-year averages — computation method ambiguityMed
Issue"5-year cumulative average" can mean cumulative-weighted (Σ CAPEX / Σ pax) or arithmetic mean of 5 annual ratios. Different result on COVID-distorted series.
Affects claims: All "5y avg" figures in raw-data and benchmark-tables
F8Cohort median sensitivityMed
IssueCohort median CAPEX/pax = A$13.5 on the ACCC-4 cohort (n=4); inclusion of ADL FY24 (A$13) lowers the median to A$11.5 (n=5). Both views are valid; "× median" comparisons should specify which cohort the median is computed on.
MethodologyLeague table presents both medians (n=4 and n=5); narrative references the n=4 ACCC-monitored median where comparing to ACCC-monitored airports specifically.
Affects claims: C001 C002 C004
F9Indicative program totals vs audited annual CAPEXMed
IssueSeveral headline figures are multi-year program-totals or indicative disclosures, not audited annual cash-flow CAPEX, and are presented as such throughout (D6 derivation code).
ExamplesHBA "~A$350m+" = sum of 4 named projects in mixed lifecycle stages; ADL "A$1bn 10-year program" wording is "indicative" per AAL Integrated Review FY22 p.8; CBR "A$3bn since 1998" includes adjacent commercial property (Brindabella, Majura, Fairbairn precincts); CNS "A$1bn" is Land Use Plan vision approved Mar 2025.
Affects claims: C003 C005 C010
F10Year-boundary ambiguityLow
Issue"FY24" = year ended 30 Jun 2024 (Australian); ACCC labels as "2023-24"; calendar-year sources (some news) say "2024" meaning Jan-Dec.
StatusVerified clean for ACCC + BITRE primary sources used in this report.
Affects claims: No headline claim affected
F11Discrepancy reconciliation incompleteMed
IssueWhere multiple sources publish the same figure, discrepancies were flagged in collector summary but not always reflected per-cell in raw-data tables.
ExamplesPER FY24 pax: ACCC 16.14m vs BITRE 12.79m (26% gap, FIFO inclusion); ADL FY24 pax: AAL 8.54m vs BITRE 8.25m (3.5% gap); BNE FY24 ATMs: ACCC 222.5k vs BITRE 188k
Affects claims: C007
F12Tertiary sources embeddedLow
IssueSome capacity figures (runway counts, terminal m²) come from Wikipedia (S16). Marked tertiary but figures still propagate.
Affects claims: Capacity sub-rows in raw-data per airport (no headline claim)
F13PDF text-extraction errors not QA'dMed
Issuepypdf can misread tables (column alignment, en-dash vs minus, parenthesised negatives). No second-eye QA on each extracted line.
StatusHighest-impact figures (CAPEX, revenue, EBITDA, total assets) spot-checked against PDF source. Residual ~2-3 of 200+ data points may have errors.
Affects claims: Marginal; all spot-checked claims validated
F14One-off items not consistently normalisedMed
IssueSeveral years had unusual items distorting comparisons.

Methodology

How to use this audit. Before quoting any figure externally:

  1. Look up the figure in the Claims section to find its claim ID (C001–C022)
  2. Confirm the source (S1–S17) and derivation (D1–D6) — direct read or computed
  3. Read the listed risks (F1–F16) — especially F3 (denominator) and F4 (EBITDA definition) for any cross-airport comparison
  4. If the claim involves "× median" — recompute against the current cohort-of-disclosure since medians shifted when ADL was added
  5. If the claim involves a multi-year average crossing FY20-FY25 — restate to real terms using ABS CPI before publishing

Strongest series in this report: the S1 ACCC supplementary database series for SYD/MEL/BNE/PER FY20-FY24 (audited regulatory accounts) and the S3 + S4 + S5 AAL FY22-FY25 cash-flow CAPEX line (audited per AASB Tier 2). Everything else carries one or more of the F1-F16 caveats.
